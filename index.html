Rolex Submariner vs. the New Yacht-Master II: Which is the Better Buy?

The Submariner needs no introduction — it has been the benchmark dive watch since 1953. The Yacht-Master II, by contrast, remains one of the most divisive models in the Rolex catalog. The 2026 update narrows the gap between them in important ways while sharpening their differences in others.

Case and wearability

The Submariner wears at 41mm with a lug-to-lug of 48.1mm and a thickness of 12.4mm. The Yacht-Master II has always been the bigger watch at 44mm, but the 2026 refresh shaves nearly a millimeter off the thickness and shortens the lugs by two millimeters. The result is a watch that still commands wrist presence but no longer feels like it is fighting smaller wrists.

Movement and complications

The Submariner runs the calibre 3230 — time-only with a date at three o'clock. The Yacht-Master II uses the calibre 4161, a regatta chronograph with a programmable countdown timer that can be set from one to ten minutes. The countdown mechanism uses a mechanical memory that remembers the programmed duration across resets — a genuinely impressive piece of engineering that most owners will never use for its intended purpose.

Investment perspective

The Submariner is the safer bet for appreciation. Its track record stretches seventy years, and every generation has appreciated after discontinuation. The Yacht-Master II is more speculative — production numbers are lower, but collector demand has historically been softer. The 2026 redesign could change that calculus if it brings new buyers into the reference.

The verdict

Buy the Submariner if you want a do-everything daily watch with ironclad resale. Buy the Yacht-Master II if you want something distinctive that stands apart at any gathering of watch enthusiasts. Neither is a wrong choice — they are answers to different questions.
